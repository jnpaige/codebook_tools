The cores exploited for small to medium-sized ﬂake production
can be divided into two technologically signiﬁcant sets. The ﬁrst set
corresponds to a type of débitage described by Delagnes and Roche
(2005) as ‘simple,’ including pieces displaying unorganised few
ﬂake scars, which are classed in the present paper as ‘modiﬁed’
pieces. This set is quantitatively more signiﬁcant within the lava
group than within the obsidian sample. Only three obsidian
modiﬁed small blocks were used for large ﬂake production. The
second set, which is quantitatively and qualitatively more signiﬁcant, is comprised of cores that exhibit organised exploitation
patterns.
