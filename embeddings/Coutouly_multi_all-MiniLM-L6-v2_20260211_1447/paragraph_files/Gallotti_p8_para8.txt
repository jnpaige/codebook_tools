Medium-sized angular, elongated, and spherical cobbles (Fig. 7:
5e7) are the most common types in the unworked sample and
they correspond to the most frequently exploited forms for small
débitage. Large cobbles and blocks (Fig. 7: 8e9) are very rare
within unworked material and they are mainly of Melka Fault lava
and pophyritic lavas, as in the (palaeo)channels (SOM, Tables 1
and 2).
