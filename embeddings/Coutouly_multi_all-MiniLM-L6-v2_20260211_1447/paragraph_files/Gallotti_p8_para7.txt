The unworked assemblage from Garba IVD is primarily composed
of small-medium cobbles, mainly spherical and elongated (Fig. 7: 1e
2), whereas angular elements, which have generally a trihedral volume (Fig. 7: 3), or small blocks (Fig. 7: 4), are rare (SOM, Tables 1 and
2). This morpho-metrical spectrum reﬂects the obsidian composition
found in Quaternary alluviums (Kieffer et al., 2004). Rare obsidian
blocks of large dimensions occur in the ancient alluviums. However,
the incidence of such blocks is insufﬁcient to enable an intensive LCT
blank production. Angular elements were frequently exploited for
small débitage in combination with small-medium cobbles. Obsidian
angular elements of different sizes resulted from the erosion of the
primary source over a large area at Balchit and the village’s surroundings. Consequently, the knappers of Garba IVD collected
obsidian pebbles, cobbles and blocks in the local streams near the site
and angular elements in the areas that surround the primary obsidian
source (at present up to 5e7 km from the site).
