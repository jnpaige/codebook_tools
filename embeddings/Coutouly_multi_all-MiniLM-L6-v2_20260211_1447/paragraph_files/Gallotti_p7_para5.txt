Obsidian The obsidian exploitation at Melka Kunture during the
Lower Pleistocene represents the ﬁrst known example of the
intense use of this high-quality raw material (Piperno et al., 2009;
Salvi et al., 2011). Although obsidian use was documented at the
late Pliocene A.L. 894 site (Hadar, Ethiopia; Goldman-Neuman
and Hovers, 2012), the obsidian production at Garba IV (not only
in the unit D but also in the lower unit E) represents the earliest
known systematic exploitation and structured chaîne opératoire
in the Lower Pleistocene assemblages (Piperno et al., 2009).
