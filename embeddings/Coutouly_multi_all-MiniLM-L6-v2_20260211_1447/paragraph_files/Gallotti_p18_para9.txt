A single débitage method is documented by the core analysis,
which resembles the unifacial centripetal prepared method
described for small-medium ﬂake production (Fig. 17: 1e2). The
horizontal plane of large cobbles or, rarely, blocks, is used as the
ﬂaking surface, whereas a peripheral or semi-peripheral plane serves
to prepare the striking platform through unidirectional removals.
The majority of these cores exhibit a single large negative scar on the
ﬂaking surface that corresponds to the production of the large ﬂake. A
small number of cores exhibit a large negative scar and a small
number of centripetal previous negative scars, which suggests the
preparation of a peripheral convexity and the predetermination of a
large and very thick ﬂake. After the detachment of the large ﬂake, the
cores are abandoned. The cores are typically 15e19 cm long and
clearly distinguishable from those focused on small-medium ﬂake
production that belong to the same method (Fig. 8).
