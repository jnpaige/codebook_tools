The percussion materials can be divided into two signiﬁcant sets
based on a combination of several patterns: percussions marks, raw
material, size, weight, and morphology. The ﬁrst set corresponds to
47 hammerstones that display areas with highly concentrated
pitting and ergonomic shapes that enable their use as hand-held
hammerstones without previous intentional morphological modiﬁcation (Fig. 18: 1e2). These hammerstones are medium-sized
spherical or elongated cobbles with plano- or bi-convex crosssections. In most cases (n ¼ 32), the impact scars are located in the
central and protruding part of a convex face opposite to a natural
and unmodiﬁed surface. In the other examples, the percussion zone
is located at one or both extremities of an elongated cobble. Only
