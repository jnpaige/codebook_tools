The emergence of the early Acheulean at Melka Kunture was
assigned by Chavaillon et al. (1979) to the lower level (J) of the
Garba XII site, dated to w1.0 Ma (Schmitt et al., 1977; Cressier,
1980): “Then, comes the Early Acheulean.The way of life, i.e.,
the location and the organization of the occupation-ﬂoors, remains
in the Oldowan tradition, but the choppers change: their angle of
utilization becomes acute; handaxes, cleavers and standardized
scrapers appear” (Chavaillon et al., 1979:22). Based on these assumptions, the early Acheulean seems to appear later at Melka
Kunture than at the other East African sites. In addition, contrary to
the dichotomy of Developed Oldowan/early Acheulean proposed by
Leakey (1971, 1975) at Olduvai not only for the time period that
concerns the earliest appearance of the Acheulean but also through
the Middle Pleistocene, at Melka Kunture “nothing suggests the
existence of two parallel lines which would have coexisted for more
than one million years on the Ethiopian high plateau” (Chavaillon
et al., 1979:23) because “notwithstanding the large number of
sites identiﬁed at Melka Kunture, and although many of them
long stratigraphic sequences.nowhere has it been
present
possible thus far to observe a sequence of Oldowan or Developed
Oldowan levels and Acheulian ones” (Piperno et al., 2004c:580).
