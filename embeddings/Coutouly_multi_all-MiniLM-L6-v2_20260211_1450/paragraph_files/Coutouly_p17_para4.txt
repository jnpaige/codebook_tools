In northwestern Alaska, one of the major sites 
with microblade technology present in good dated 
stratigraphic context is Onion Portage. It contains different occupation phases, some of them 
with microblade assemblages such as the Akmak 
(ca. 8700 BC) and Kobuk (ca. 7000 BC) complexes 
(Anderson 1970, 1988). Based on published 
illustrations, the cores from this site range from 
small wedge-shaped specimens to larger prismatic 
ones; whether the differences in core morphology 
represent different technical traditions or adaptation to raw materials is a question that should be 
addressed in the future.
