Raw material provisioning at Garba IVD was based solely on
local secondary sources.
In West Turkana, medium-grained
phonolite was the predominant rock type at Kokiselei 4, both for
small débitage and LCT production. At Kokiselei 5, the majority of
the small débitage consisted of small- to medium-sized cobbles
(angular and rounded) or fragments of cobbles of the mediumgrained phonolite and, to a lesser extent, lower quality cobbles or
fragments of cobbles of aphyritic or porphyritic basalts, all forms
available from the nearby conglomerates (Harmand, 2009a). At
Kokiselei 4, the LCT blanks are large ﬂat or elongated cobbles, split
or whole, and, rarely, large ﬂakes. At Kokiselei 5, the blank of the
only LCT is a large split cobble. Cobbles and blocks of such dimensions are poorly represented in the nearby conglomerates. The
selection in stream channels and/or bedrock outcrops was assessed
by Harmand (2009a). This provisioning modality has been documented in West Turkana since the end of the Pliocene at Lokalalei
2C (Harmand, 2009b), but the selection shifts to rocks that are best
suited to the more varied templates at Kokiselei 4 in terms of
quality and morphologies.
