As previously mentioned, small débitage dominates the Garba
IVD assemblage and LCT production is a minor component. The cooccurrence of these two chaînes opératoires is not systematic, as
documented at Nyabusosi 18 in Uganda (Texier, 1995, 2005), the ST
Complex in Peninj (de la Torre et al., 2003), Kokiselei 5 in West
Turkana (Texier et al., 2006), and at several sites in Olduvai (Leakey,
1971; de la Torre and Mora, 2005) and in Koobi Fora (Isaac and
Isaac, 1997; Braun et al., 2008b). Although the production of LCTs
became a major component of the Acheulean lithic record during
the end of the Lower and the Middle Pleistocene in East Africa,
assemblages with no or rare LCTs have also been described during
this period in the same area in the Middle Awash (de Heinzelin
et al., 2002; Schick and Clark, 2003), at Nadung’a 4 in West Turkana (Delagnes et al., 2006), Mwanganda’s Village (Clark and
Haynes, 1970; Surovell et al., 2005), Chesowanja (Gowlett et al.,
1981) and Olorgesailie (Isaac, 1977).
