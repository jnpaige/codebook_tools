Two cleavers (sensu Tixier, 1956) were recovered at Garba IVD:
one of Melka Fault lava, the other of porphyritic basalt. The cleaver
edge morphology was determined prior to the detachment of the
cleaver blank by removals whose directions are perpendicular
(Fig. 17: 7) or oblique (Fig. 17: 8) to that of the cleaver blank. A
shaping process was adopted in only one case (Fig. 17: 7) to reduce
the irregular convexity of the dorsal face of the ﬂake blank and thin
the butt-bulb part of the ventral face. Retouch does not intervene to
regularise edges that display a denticulate and irregular form. The
second example was not shaped but only retouched on the dorsal
face (Fig. 17: 8).
