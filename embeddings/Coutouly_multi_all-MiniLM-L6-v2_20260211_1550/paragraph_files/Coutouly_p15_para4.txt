In the Amakomanak assemblage, microblade thickness appears to be the most significant 
attribute. In the experimental data from Pelegrin, 
which I have personally studied (Gómez Coutouly 
2011a), microblades over 2 mm thick are quite rare 
during Mode 1 and Mode 2 microblade removals 
(less than 20%), while they are very common in 
pressure- flaked Mode 3 microblades (over 70%). 
In the case of the Amakomanak microblades, 
over 60% of microblades are thicker than 2 mm 
(Table 2). Amakomanak also has a few microblades with a thickness over 3 mm, which also is 
compatible with a Mode 2 or 3 reduction, but not 
with simple handheld pressure flaking (based on 
experimental data).
