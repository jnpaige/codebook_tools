Products obtained using the centripetal ﬂaking direction are
not frequent. They show sections with a certain degree of
symmetry, sub-quadrangular or circular shapes, and dihedral
or facetted butts (Fig. 14: 13, 15). Flakes with a ﬂaking angle
between 95(cid:3) and 100(cid:3) that frequently display dihedral or
facetted butts with unidirectional negative removals are most
likely issued from unifacial centripetal prepared cores (Fig. 14:
15). Generally, the ﬂakes display a tangential ﬂaking direction
with a transversal asymmetric cross-section (Fig. 14: 14) and a
complete or partial back formed by the core edge (pseudoLevallois points; Fig. 14: 16e17).
