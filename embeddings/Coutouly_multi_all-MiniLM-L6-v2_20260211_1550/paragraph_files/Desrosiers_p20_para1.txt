Archie prehistory concerns the methods
 used to produce schist tools (e.g., Clark
 1982; Dumond 1968, 1977: 63). In the
 Eastern Arctic, several authors have
 provided detailed or fragmentary
 descriptions of the chaîne opératoire of
 schist tool production (ACI 1993: 82-85;
 Benmouyal 1978: 68-73; Harp 1964:
 62-64; Weetaluktuk n.d.) . Characteristic
 grooved waste fragments from schist
 tool production are now well known,
 but they are still confused with abraders
 or sharpeners (Auger 1986: 116; Nagy
 2000: 139). At GhGk-63, for example, a
 combination of chipping and grooving
 operations is associated with the schist
 artifacts and debitage. Resolving this
 debate will require careful examination
 and comparison of the lithic assemblages
 from many sites.
