With regard to LCT production, all LCTs at Garba IVD, except for
one retouched core, were made from large ﬂakes. Garba IVD offers
the rare opportunity to analyse large cores for large ﬂake production, commonly absent at other sites, with the exception of two
large cores from RHS-Mugulud in Peninj. This suggests that the
temporal and spatial fragmentation of the LCT reduction process
that has been inferred to be typically Acheulean (de la Torre and
Mora, 2005; Goren-Inbar and Sharon, 2006; de la Torre et al.,
2008; de la Torre, 2011a) is absent at Garba IVD. The large ﬂake
in situ production at Garba IVD could explain the relative number of
un-retouched large ﬂakes present in the assemblage. Only ﬂake
blanks for cleaver production could document a fragmentation of
the chaîne opératoire, but the negligible number does not permit
drawing conclusions. The location and accessibility of the primary
and secondary raw material sources, the occurrence and the frequency of proper modules for the different knapping activities, and
the site formation processes are some of the aspects that could have
separately or collectively produced such inter-site variability.
