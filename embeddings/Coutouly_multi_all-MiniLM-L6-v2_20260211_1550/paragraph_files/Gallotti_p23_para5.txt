At the present time, no detailed data are available for smallmedium ﬂake production at Kokiselei 4, where only LCTs were
the object of a technological analysis (Chevrier, 2012). At Kokiselei
5, débitage systems appear to be less constrained by the initial
morphology of the raw materials and they are unifacial or bifacial
alternating (Texier et al., 2006). The same variability documented at
Garba IVD also characterises the small débitage in Peninj. It is
interesting to note that hierarchical centripetal exploitation is the
most common strategy used by the Peninj hominins. As argued by
de la Torre et al. (2008), the adoption of this method is relevant in
cultural terms because the same technological knowledge seems to
be shared by knappers both in the ST complex and in RHS-Mugulud
and MHS-Bayasi, regardless of the presence of LCTs.
