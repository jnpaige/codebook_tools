Raynal et al. (2004) suggested two hypotheses concerning the
deposition of this unit. The ﬁrst hypothesis excludes anthropic
intervention: it is the case of a total reworking, when a ﬂood
simultaneously transported archaeological and natural materials.
The second hypothesis considers that hominins settled on the lag
deposit, where they used the raw material available on the spot to
manufacture lithic tools after the water receded. All of the elements
abandoned at the site were partly reworked when the sands of the
upper sub-unit (bed 3.2; Fig. 2c) deposited.
