A classiﬁcation of the cores, which is particularly informative in
the assessment of knapping methods and techniques, was performed using the identiﬁcation of the number of ﬂaking surfaces,
the direction of ﬂaking, the presence or absence of a distinct prepared striking platform, and the value of the angle between the
striking platform and the ﬂaking surface. Considering these attributes, the core analysis facilitated the identiﬁcation of exploitation
modalities, the management of volumes and the presence or
absence of a hierarchical organisation of the surfaces.
