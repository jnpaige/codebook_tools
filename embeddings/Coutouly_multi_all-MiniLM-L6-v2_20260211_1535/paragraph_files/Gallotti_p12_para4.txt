The number of ﬂakes is highly limited compared with the
number of negative scars recorded during the core analysis. Core
fragments aside, there is a core to ﬂake ratio of 2:1, which does not
ﬁt with the mean number of negative scars that can be observed
on the cores. Additionally, most cores have been intensively
exploited, and most ﬂakes exhibit a large number of negative scars
on the dorsal face, which suggests that the detached piece count
does not reﬂect the number of ﬂakes that were originally obtained
from the cores. In any case, ﬂakes represent all of the ﬂaking
stages and methods identiﬁed in the core analysis (Fig. 12). Thus,
the large deﬁcit of ﬂakes could be interpreted to result from a
winnowing by natural agents rather than from a spatial and
temporal fragmentation of this chaîne operatoire.
