Impact traits are the principal criterion of distinction with the
previous group but not the only criterion. First, there is no strict
selection of speciﬁc raw material: 25 specimens are of aphyric
rocks, nine are of porphyritic basalt, three of microdoleritic basalt,
and one of trachybasalt. In addition, the specimens are larger and
substantially heavier than the hammerstones used for knapping
(SOM, Table 3). Moreover, they are not homogeneous in size and
weight and exhibit irregular forms that are primarily attributable to
angular cobbles or small blocks.
