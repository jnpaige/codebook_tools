Most Kapthurin Formation Acheulian Levallois ﬂakes and cores preserve
traces of a centripetal ﬂake removal pattern. Bidirectional ﬂaking was observed
on only a single ﬂake and core, the latter on a thin tabular lava boulder, whose
form likely dictated the ﬂake removal pattern (see Fig. 3f). Most cobbles and
boulders in Kapthurin Formation conglomerates are rounded to sub-rounded in
shape. Striking platforms are facetted.
