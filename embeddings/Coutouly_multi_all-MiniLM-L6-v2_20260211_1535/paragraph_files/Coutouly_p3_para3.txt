The Amakomanak Site 
(Noatak National Preserve, 
Northwest Alaska)
The Amakomanak site (AMR-00095) is located in 
northwestern Alaska near the headwaters of the 
Amakomanak Creek in the Cutler River drainage. 
It was discovered as part of a National Park Service 
(NPS) survey program in 1992, and the investigation was carried out by Robert Gal. In 2003, a new 
series of investigations led to the identification 
of 21 localities at the site, two of them (G and N) 
with a microblade industry (Gal et al. 2004, 2005). 
Three samples recovered from a buried context 
during testing were AMS 14C to ca. 8700 to 8650 
uncal. BP (Gal et al. 2005)—ca. 7650 to 7450 BC at 
2σ using OxCal 4.3.2 (Bronk Ramsey 2009) with 
IntCal 13 (Reimer et al. 2013)—consistent with an
