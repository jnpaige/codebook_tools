The crystal quartz microblades (n=38)
 are smaller, thinner, and narrower than
 their chert counterparts. They also
 exhibit greater parallelism of arris and
 edges (good: 10, medium: 2, low: 7,
 and the remainder are undetermined) ,
 a short bulb, and well-shaped blanks.
 The majority of microblades shows little
 standardisation, which may be linked
 both to the difficulty of flaking crystal
 quartz, and to the small size of the crys tals; average size is 35 mm by 20 mm.
