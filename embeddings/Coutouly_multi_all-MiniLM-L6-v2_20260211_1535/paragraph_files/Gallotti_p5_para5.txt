Each analysed lithic object (knapped and unworked) was lithologically characterised to infer the raw material composition and
the provisioning and exploitation patterns as a potential source of
archaeological assemblage variability. Additionally, unworked objects were classiﬁed in terms of natural shape and size to appraise
the range of available shapes and dimensions, which could facilitate
the initial lithic production phases and play a role in the choice of a
speciﬁc production activity or débitage method.
