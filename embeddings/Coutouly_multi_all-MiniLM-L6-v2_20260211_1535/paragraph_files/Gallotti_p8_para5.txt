The welded ignimbrite N.1 is well represented in the alluvium
system and it accounts for 25.6% of the unworked sample (Fig. 6).
This porous, non-homogeneous and non-compact rock is unsuitable for knapping, and it has been rarely used by knappers (Fig. 6).
Melka Fault lava is another common rock within the unworked
material (Fig. 6). This aphyric ﬂuidal lava, which is associated with
the limit fault (south-southeast) of the Melka Kunture basin, is
notable for its abundance in the (palaeo)landscape. The lava often
appears in alluvial deposits with the vesicles oriented in the direction of the ﬂuidal structure. Most rarely, the lava’s peculiar
ﬂuidal texture can be highly compact and thus adequate for
knapping. The lava’s vesiculated facies occurs frequently in unworked material, and only 4.9% corresponds to the lava’s compact
facies. In contrast, knappers selected only compact facies for lithic
production (Fig. 6).
