The second set is composed of hammerstones with fracture
angles (sensu Mora and de la Torre, 2005). These tools (n ¼ 38)
exhibit heavily battered ridges and numerous superimposed small
negative scars that are irregular in shape with an impact point in
their central portion and without a set directionality (Fig. 18: 3e5).
These patterns prevent consideration of these pieces as regular
hammerstones typically used for knapping, as argued elsewhere
(de la Torre and Mora, 2005; Mora and de la Torre, 2005; de la Torre,
2011a).
