Besides, the presence and frequency of these two chaînes
opératoires within 1.76e1.40 Ma East African assemblages
demonstrate important intra- and inter-site variability. In West
Turkana, at Kokiselei 4, which recently pushed the ﬁrst appearance for Acheulean technology back to 1.76 Ma (Lepre et al.,
2011), handaxes or proto-handaxes and picks, along with small
ﬂake production, represent approximately 15% of the assemblage
(28 of 191 items; Chevrier, 2012). Conversely, at Kokiselei 5, dated
to approximately 1.65 Ma (Roche et al., 2003), the lithic assemblage (1766 items) is entirely devoted to small-medium ﬂake
production, with only one LCT (Texier et al., 2006). The same
variability has been documented in Peninj around 1.6e1.4 Ma
(Domínguez-Rodrigo et al., 2002; de la Torre et al., 2003, 2008;
Mora et al., 2003). Eleven archaeological sites in the ST Complex
in Peninj yielded lithic series (354 items) completely focused on
small ﬂake production. At
the same time, LCT production
constituted an important set at MHS-Bayasi (21 of 168 items) and
RHS-Mugulud (97 of 508 items). Several sites in Olduvai yielded
assemblages with few LCTs (de la Torre and Mora, 2005). At EFHR (w1.6e1.5 Ma; Manega, 1993), 7% of the assemblage (429
items) was represented by LCTs (n ¼ 29) and LCT blanks (n ¼ 2).
There were two LCTs at the FC West Occupation Floor (w1.6e
1.5 Ma; Manega, 1993) within an assemblage of 1201 objects; the
rich assemblages of TK Lower and Upper Floors (w1.50e1.33 Ma;
de la Torre and Mora, 2005), of respectively 2325 and 5268
specimens, yielded 10 and 17 LCTs. A small number of LCTs
(n ¼ 3) were found at Gadeb 2E within an assemblage of 761
pieces. The only site known in the Western Great Rift, Nyabusosi
18 (w1.5 Ma), yielded a lithic assemblage (600 items) lacking LCTs
(Texier, 1995, 2005).
