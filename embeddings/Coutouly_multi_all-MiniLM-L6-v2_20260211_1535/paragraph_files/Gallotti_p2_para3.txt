In 1967, Chavaillon discovered the ﬁrst Oldowan evidence in
Melka Kunture at the Gombore I site and in the following years at
Karre I, Garba IV and Gombore Ig (Chavaillon, 1967; Chavaillon and
Chavaillon, 1969, 1971; Chavaillon et al., 1979; Berthelet and
Chavaillon, 2004; Chavaillon and Berthelet, 2004). At this time, the
Garba IVD industry, dated to 1.5 Ma, was attributed to the Developed
Oldowan based on the high frequency of retouched ﬂakes, the variety of chopper forms, the quantitative importance of polyhedrons
and the presence of few bifaces and cleavers (Chavaillon and
Piperno, 1975; Piperno and Bulgarelli-Piperno, 1975).
