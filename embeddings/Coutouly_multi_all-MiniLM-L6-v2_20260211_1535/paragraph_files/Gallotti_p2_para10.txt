Archaeostratigraphic unit D is the richest level in the Garba IV
sequence with 19,055 ﬁnds (9821 lithic artefacts, 6654 unworked
lithic objects and 2580 faunal remains; Gallotti and Piperno, 2003,
2004), which were recorded systematically over approximately
100 m2 (Fig. 3a). Unit D appears as a carpet of lithic artefacts and
faunal remains (Fig. 3b, c) embedded in sediments composed of
sand and ﬁne gravel and numerous unmodiﬁed pebbles, cobbles
and blocks. Lithic objects (knapped and unworked) present
different levels of abrasion. However, only 25.7% of the objects are
in a fresh condition, and in certain cases (2.3%) fresh surfaces and
edges coexist with abraded areas on the same object.
