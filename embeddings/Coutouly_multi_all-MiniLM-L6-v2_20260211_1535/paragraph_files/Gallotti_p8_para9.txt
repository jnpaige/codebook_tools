A total of 1780 cores, 107 core fragments, 3570 ﬂakes and 193
retouched ﬂakes document the small-medium ﬂake production.
Obsidian and aphyric rocks are the most exploited raw materials
both for cores and ﬂakes (Fig. 5).
Cores The core analysis documents both simple and organised
débitage. Core size reﬂects the dimensions of the natural forms
documented within unworked material (SOM, Table 2) and in
Quaternary alluviums (Kieffer et al., 2004). Size distribution is
rather homogeneous and no relevant differences are visible
according to a particular débitage method. Largest cores belong
to simple débitage cores, given the low number of detached
pieces (Fig. 8).
