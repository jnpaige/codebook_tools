The recent new data from East Africa and the current debate
require a redeﬁnition of the identity of the Lower Pleistocene lithic
assemblages from Melka Kunture and the consequent cognitive and
behavioural implications. This paper updates the available information on the Garba IVD lithic assemblage, which represents one of
the richest Early Stone Age lithic series in East Africa (Chavaillon
and Piperno, 1975; Piperno and Bulgarelli-Piperno, 1975;
Chavaillon et al., 1979; Piperno, 1980, 2001). The results of the
techno-economic revision in terms of manual dexterity and the
technical achievements of the lithic series argue for the relevance of
Garba IVD and demonstrate an older origin for the Acheulean at
Melka Kunture, 0.5 Ma than previously inferred (Chavaillon and
Berthelet, 2004).
