Presently, it is difficult to establish the 
presence of a well- defined microblade technological complex in northwestern Alaska, since the 
known assemblages mainly correspond to various 
time periods or come from undated or surface 
contexts. But the fact that these large prismatic 
cores are seen across this region at different time 
periods may well correspond to an adaptation to 
local raw materials, rather than a regional/cultural 
variant. This is especially the case when wedgeshaped Campus- style microblade cores are present 
in some sites and sometimes in the assemblages 
where prismatic cores are to be found, which is not 
the case for the Sumnagin complex where virtually 
no wedge-shaped cores are produced once they 
shift towards the production of conical microblade 
cores after the Paleolithic Dyuktai complex.
