One of the main differences between the 
Amakomanak collection and most of the Late 
Pleistocene and Early Holocene microblade assemblages from the Russian Far East, Siberia, and Central Alaska that I have studied (Gómez Coutouly 
2011a) resides in the pressure- flaking mode. While 
most of these assemblages have a microblade- 
reduction process based on a handheld/shoulder- 
crutch type of pressure, the metric information 
(width, thickness, and length of microblades) from 
the Amakomanak microblade component indicates 
that a pressure- flaking mode similar to a Mode 3 
may have been used.
