Two major elements are characteristic of the cultural change
that led to the origin of the Acheulean in East Africa: (1) the
emergence of a new chaîne opératoire focused on large ﬂake/LCT
production linked to a strict selection of proper modules of speciﬁc
raw materials and to prepared débitage methods, and (2) a large
variability of small débitage modalities with systematic preparation
of the striking platform and the appearance of a certain degree of
predetermination. These technical innovations are shared by other
penecontemporaneous East African sites (Isaac, 1986; Texier, 1995,
2005; de la Torre et al., 2008; de la Torre, 2011a).
