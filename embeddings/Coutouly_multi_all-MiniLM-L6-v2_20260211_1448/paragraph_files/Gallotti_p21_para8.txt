- Adaptation to the original morphology of cobbles to maximise the
production of ﬂakes without speciﬁc patterns. The peripheral
unidirectional method shows the use of the maximal extension
of the convex surface to detach ﬂakes and the adoption of the
only ﬂat surface of the cobble as the striking platform. Système
par Surface de Débitage Alternée (SSDA) represents a modality
to produce a high number of ﬂakes exploiting the original geometry of the cobbles and preserving angular planes. In most
cases, multifacial multidirectional irregular exploitation did
not modify the original form of the blank, irregularly using any
available ﬂaking angles without striking platform preparation
to produce the maximum number of ﬂakes possible.
