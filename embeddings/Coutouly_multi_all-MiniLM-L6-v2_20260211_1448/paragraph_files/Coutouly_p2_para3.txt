Microblade technology is a major aspect of the 
prehistory of Alaska and Beringia. So far, it is the 
earliest recognized technology in Alaska, since 
pressure- flaked microblade cores are the main production at Swan Point CZ4b, the earliest known 
human presence in Alaska dating to ca. 12500 BC 
(Holmes 2011). The technique of pressure flaking 
microblades appeared in the archaeological record 
about 20,000 years ago in northeast Asia, before 
diffusing towards other regions (Russian Far East, 
Siberia, Alaska, and Canada, as well as Central 
Asia) (Gómez Coutouly 2011b, in press). This technology is well-known in Alaska, in different areas 
and at different time periods. It is the hallmark 
of the Late Pleistocene Denali Complex of central
