The ﬂaked surface corresponds in 43.8% of the cases to the
longest natural face of the original support, exploited over its
entire length to produce one to two series of three to nine
elongated ﬂakes from a striking platform rectiﬁed by removals
to create a suitable angle between 78(cid:3) and 88(cid:3) (Fig. 10: 1e4).
Only six of these cores document the adoption of the bipolar
technique (Fig. 10: 5).
Flat cobbles were ﬂaked on their sagittal plane (9%), which
presented a favourable convexity for knapping from a rectiﬁed
striking platform. At discard, the ﬂaked surfaces frequently
present a last series of hinged ﬂake scars, which suggests that
the knappers could not continue to exploit the piece due to the
limited extension of its convexity (Fig. 10: 6).
